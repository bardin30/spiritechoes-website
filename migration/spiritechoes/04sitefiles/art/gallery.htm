"Sway Dresser"
by Wolf Sittler		 Wolf Sittler... Focusing on Form & Function

Wolf Sittler began wood working over 33 years ago and now focuses on creating "functional" sculpture. By utilizing a blend of form, function, wood grain and color, Sittler takes his carefully selected wood and creates furniture that truly enhances the living and working environment.

Working exclusively with native woods, from pine to maple burl, Wolf has had a special interest in wood chairs since 1987. Expounding on his process, Sittler states, "Many believe that wood chairs cannot be comfortable but experience has taught me this is not so. By shaping the back and seat to conform to the natural body structure, a wooden chair can provide lasting comfort."

Over the years, Sittler's work has evolved from simple linear shapes and angular patterns to a current emphasis on curves and sculptural forms. His products include desks, game tables, sofa tables, chairs of all kinds, dressers, clocks and other custom designed work. Truly unique, his functional furnishings are indeed sculptural and are meant to be viewed, used,. . .and caressed.

"Sun Signs"
by Reeda Peel		 Reeda Peel... Preserving Ancient Messages

An artist and avocational archeologist, Reeda Peel has combined her artistic talent and archeological interests to create a unique look into Native American pre-history.

Peel enjoys portraying scenes of the ancient Americans' every day life as well as classical interpretations of their rock art. The scenes, created from the artist's imagination, are based on careful research of archeological data, the writings of early Spanish explorers, and written information on the cultures existing today.

Working in the ancient Native American shelters of the lower Pecos Region, Reeda Peel meticulously draws her interpretation of the painted images before her on the rock walls, contemplating their meaning. Visions come to her from her travels to the lower Pecos Region of South Texas and through hiking expeditions. "Sun Signs" pictured is an interpretation of Apache rock art from the E1 Paso region.
Annis Allen... Recycling Discarded Treasures

Gaze at the contemporary abstract works of Annis Allen and you may see color, form, an abstract canyon or sea coastline... a closer look may reveal a bit of copper, a piece of vintage glass, a label from an Oriental shipment of goods, or a piece of tissue from a local department store. Creating beauty from mundane materials becomes a challenge and delight for Annis as she layers her "treasures' into a one-of-a-kind works of lasting beauty.

An artist for 25 years, Ms. Allen lived and worked in the Caribbean and the northeast before returning to native Texas in 1980. She now resides in Austin with her husband, artist R.T. Allen. A hearty congratulations to this couple who has just celebrated their fiftieth wedding anniversary this past year !

Many of Annis' works have been selected for special exhibitions in the Society of Layerists Multimedia Shows. She is a signature member of the Texas Watercolor Society and an active member of the Texas Fine Arts Association, and the Austin Contemporary Visual Arts Association.

 Marianne Caroselli... Creating Art is her Business

Marianne Caroselli began creating art at age ten.  Little did she know that this would turn into a life - long inspiration.

Caroselli began her oil painting career in 1972 after her family moved to Texas.  Before long Mary was approached by the prestigious card company, Leanin' Tree, to reproduce her work on cards, posters and other gift items.  Calendar companies began to call to inquire about placing her work on their products, and soon Marianne's career was taking off.

Multi - talented Caroselli began to sculpt in 1979.   Professional classes soon followed at the Cowboy Artists of America Museum in Kerrville.  Today, Marianne's sculptures range from table size to life size images of children, cowboys, Native Americans, horses and wildlife, just to name a few.  Her work can be found in many corporate collections as well as the private collections of Burt Reynolds, Daniel Stern, Wayne Newton and Byron Nelson.

Represented by galleries throughout Texas, New Mexico, Arizona, Colorado and Florida, Marianne keeps busy supplying al her representatives with work... but then, creating art is her business and she enjoys every minute of it.
